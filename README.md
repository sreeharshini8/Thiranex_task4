# End-to-End Online Retail Customer Segmentation and Sales Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing an online retail dataset to uncover customer purchasing behavior, sales trends, and business insights using Python.  

The project also implements **Customer Segmentation** using **RFM Analysis** and **K-Means Clustering** to group customers based on their purchasing patterns.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Perform data cleaning and preprocessing
- Conduct Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Analyze customer purchasing behavior
- Identify sales trends and revenue patterns
- Perform Customer Segmentation using Machine Learning
- Generate business insights and recommendations

---

## 📂 Dataset Information
The dataset contains transactional records of an online retail store.

### Features Included:
- InvoiceNo
- StockCode
- Description
- Quantity
- InvoiceDate
- UnitPrice
- CustomerID
- Country

## 🤖 Customer Segmentation

Customer segmentation was performed using:

### RFM Analysis
- Recency
- Frequency
- Monetary Value

### K-Means Clustering
Customers were grouped into different segments such as:
- Loyal Customers
- High-Value Customers
- Regular Customers
- Inactive Customers

## 📈 Key Insights

- The United Kingdom generated the highest sales and transactions.
- A small group of customers contributed significantly to total revenue.
- Certain products showed consistently high demand.
- Sales activity varied across different months.
- Customer segmentation helped identify valuable customer groups.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Provide loyalty rewards to high-value customers.
- Re-engage inactive customers through targeted promotions.
- Focus marketing efforts on top-performing regions.
- Maintain inventory for highly demanded products.
- Use customer segmentation for personalized marketing strategies.
